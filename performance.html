Exercise

Overview

The one prescription that we tend to neglect is exercise. Exercise is key to longevity, because it supports and maintains many of our physiological needs. Exercise helps prevent a myriad of diseases including the number one killer in the United States, heart disease. By subscribing to a consistent exercise routine, we can avoid early mortality or even a heavy price tag associated with healthcare.

Prevention and Reduction Heart Disease

Physical activity is perhaps one of the best preventative solutions to heart disease. General benefits that adults experience with consistent exercise is reduced body fat, reduced blood pressure, reduced low-density lipoprotein (LDL, or better known as bad cholesterol), increased high-density lipoprotein (HDL or good cholesterol), and increased insulin sensitivity. Overall, exercise is good for general health and preventing early mortality as exemplified by the graph above.

Osteoporosis Prevention

As we age, our bone density begins to deteriorate and cause osteoporosis, especially in women post menopause. Exercise, particularly resistance training, helps increasing bone density. As a first line of defense, many of us should consider exercise the primary solution.

Alternative Therapy Depression

One of the best tools for fighting depression is exercise! 1 out of 10 Americans suffer an episode of depression anually, and a lot of Americans are guided to a pharmaceutical approach for treatment. As shown in the graph above, exercise appears to be a better solution to depression than the often prescribed antidepressants. No one should rule any type of treatments of course, but maybe we should start putting more emphasis on exercise as a viable alternative to the conventional pharmacological approach.
